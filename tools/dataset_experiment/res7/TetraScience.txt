Cookies and Tracking Technologies.
Like many websites and applications, TetraScience uses cookies and other tracking technologies to help tailor the Website and Services to the needs and interests of users, to improve the user experience, as well as for systems administration, troubleshooting, and security purposes.
A cookie is a small text file stored on your device or computer when you access the Website and Services.
These small text files allow the website or application to recognize your computer or device at the next visit and remember your preferences and information.
Cookies may also be used by a website to collect data about user activity or how you use the Website and Services.
To develop information about activity related to the Website and Services, TetraScience may also use a small file, called a web beacon.
Web beacons do not by themselves collect any information from users, but in combination with cookies, web beacons allow us to track, for example, whether web pages are viewed, how often web pages are viewed, whether you opened an email we sent, the specific times of day web pages or emails are viewed.
To learn more about management of cookies and other tracking technologies, see the Your Choices section below.
