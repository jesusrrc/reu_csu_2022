Privacy Policy.
As a service provider, we value our visitors and clients and respect their                right to privacy.
Therefore, we have designed a privacy policy to explain how their ‘Personally                Identifiable Information’ is being used online.
It is our humble request for everyone to read the entire privacy policy carefully to understand how we                collect, use, and protect their information with our website.
For instance, what information do we collect as soon as you sign up for our blogs, newsletters, or                perhaps fill up an inquiry form.
Information You Provide.
As soon as you register on our website, you may be asked to enter the                following personal information.
Name.
Email.
Phone Number.
While filling out an inquiry form or a feedback form,.
for instance, you’ll be asked to enter the above personal information.
This information                helps us to provide a personalized experience for our service.
IP Address.
The site may access your device’s location using your Internet Protocol (IP)                address.
This information helps us to follow your country’s data compliance regulations to their                fullest.
We may                also use your IP address to determine the fastest route for your device to connect to our website.
How do We Use Your Information.
The information, we collect from our visitors and clients is                used in the following manner.
To personalize the user experience by delivering the type of content and services as per your                    interests.
To notify you regarding special offers on services via sending periodic emails.
To send you reminders and confirmations as per your chosen services.
To send you technical notices, updates, alerts and support messages.
To respond to your queries, feedback, and comments to provide better customer service and support.
To notify you of the latest services, offers, news posts, and blogs, which we think will interest                    you.
To analyze and manage the activities with our content and website’s performance.
Email Management.
RV Technologies may try to contact you via emails, which may happen due to                several reasons.
For instance, when you subscribe to our newsletters, you may receive periodic emails                regarding                the content update.
Emails, which you’ll receive from RV Technologies will always contain a link in                the                footer, where you can unsubscribe altogether.
You can also select your communication preferences using                the same                link.
When do We Share and Disclose Your Information.
Our visitor’s privacy is our priority, and                therefore, at every step, we make sure to protect it.
We only share your personal information under one                of the                following circumstances.
With your consent.
When you allow RV Technologies to share your personal information.
It may                happen when you submit your information through our offers or services.
For instance, if you decide                to fill                out a query form, your name, email address, and company name will be collected by RV Technologies.
Legal Obligations.
RV Technologies may also disclose your personal information when it is                required to satisfy a law, regulation, legal process, or governmental request.
It may also happen when                there                needs to be an investigation carried out regarding violation of the Term of Use, or protect the rights,                or                safety of RV Technologies and its users.
Preventing security frauds is yet another scenario where                information                disclosure may be required.
Business transfers.
If RV Technologies is involved in a merger, acquisition, or any form of                transfer or sale of some or all of its business, we may share your personal information in connection                with the                transaction itself.
What Security Measures We Take to Protect Visitor’s Information.
To protect your information,                a regular scan is carried out for security breaches and vulnerabilities to make your visit as safe as                possible.
We also use SSL certification to make sure that your every transaction is safe and secured.
Google Ad Policies.
We use Google AdSense to advertise on our website.
Google’s ads are always                displayed as per the Google Advertising Principles.
.
Google, being a third-party vendor utilize cookies to display ads on our website.
The DART cookie allows                Google                to only serve relevant ads as per the user’s interests.
Users may opt out of the use of the DART cookie                by                visiting the Google ad and content network privacy policy.
We have implemented the following.
Remarketing with Google AdSense.
Demographics and Interests Reporting.
We along with third-party vendors, such as Google use first-party cookies and third-party.
cookies together to compile data regarding user interactions with ad impressions, and other ad service                functions as they visit our website.
How Can You Opt Out.
In case you want to set preferences to Google displaying ads to you, you                can visit the Google ad setting page.
You can also permanently opt out by adding the Google Analytics                Opt-out                browser add-on.
Do Not Track Signals.
We honor Do Not Track Signals, and once we receive a Do Not Track                Signal, we do not use advertising at all.
COPPA (Children Online Privacy Protection Act).
Parents are put in control for the collection                of personal information from children under 13.
The Federal Trade Commission, the nation’s consumer                protection                agency, enforces the COPPA Rule, which states what operations websites and online services should do to                protect                children’s privacy and safety online.
Furthermore, we do not advertise to children under 13.
Fair Information Practices.
To be in line with the Fair Information Practices law, we’ll take                the following actions as soon as a data breach occurs.
We will notify the user via an email regarding the same within one business day.
We also agree to the individual redress principle, which requires that every                individual has a right to pursue legally enforceable rights against data collectors and processors who                fail to                follow the law itself.
Individuals also have all the resources to trigger a legal investigation against                data                processors.
CAN-SPAM Act.
The CAN-SPAM act is a law which establishes guidelines for sending commercial                emails, and allows the users to stop the emails which are being sent to them.
Failure on doing so is                considered                as a violation and penalties may apply.
To be in accordance with CAN-SPAM we agree to the following.
We never send misleading subjects through our emails.
We include the physical address of our office headquarters.
In case of an opt-out/Do Not Track signal, we make sure, the user does not receive emails from us at                    all.
We attach a link at the end of every email from where the user can unsubscribe.
