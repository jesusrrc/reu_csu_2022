Your Rights.
You have the right to make a complaint at any time to the Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO), the UK supervisory authority for data protection issues.
We would, however, appreciate the chance to deal with your concerns before you approach the ICO so please contact us in the first instance at [email protected].
Under certain circumstances, by law, you have the right to.
a) request erasure of Personal Data, if there is no other lawful or legitimate reason for us to continuing holding it.
b) request access to your Personal Data (commonly known as a ‘data subject access request’).
c) request correction of Personal Data that we hold relating to you or business(es).
d) request the transfer of Personal Data to a third party.
e) request the restriction of data processing, for example if you are awaiting more information about the lawful use of your Personal Data.
If you decide to exercise any of these rights, it may impact or prevent our ability to fulfil our obligation to provide you with the services.
If you want to exercise any of these rights please contact [email protected].
Under the GDPR you have the right to request access to your personal data.
You will not have to pay a fee to access your personal data (or to exercise any of the other rights you have under the GDPR).
However we may charge a reasonable sum of £10 if your request is considered unfounded, repetitive or excessive.
Alternatively, we may refuse your request in these circumstances.
In order to access your personal data we may need to request specific information from you to help us confirm your identity and ensure your right to access your data (or to exercise any of the other rights).
This is a security measure that we have implemented to ensure that personal data is not disclosed to any person who has no right to receive it.
We aim to respond to all reasonable requests within one month.
