<p>We may disclose your personal data, to the extent permitted by law, for Wapice Ltd’s internal use in the sale/marketing of products/services.</p>
<h3>10. Transfer of data outside the EU or EEA</h3>
<p>No personal data will be transferred outside the EU or EEA.</p>
<h3>11. Personal data protection policy</h3>
<p>In its role as controller, Wapice uses appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data against unauthorised or unlawful processing and against damage and loss.</p>
<p>Manually processed data</p>
<ul>
<li>Personal data is protected against unauthorised access and unlawful processing (e.g. destruction, modification or disclosure). The processors are only allowed to process personal data they need to do their jobs.</li>
<li>Any documents are stored in a locked space protected from outside parties. Documents are only printed when needed, and printouts are destroyed after use.</li>
</ul>
<p>Data processed automatically</p>
<ul>
<li>All data processing is based on access rights that depend on the person’s role and position in the organisation and, where applicable, permits granted by the person responsible for the data file. The validity of the access rights is checked daily.</li>
<li>IT systems and services are protected against unauthorised access in accordance with good practices in the field, their operational capability is secured to the extent necessary, and their life cycle is controlled.</li>
</ul>
<h3>12. Automated decisions</h3>
<p>Automated decisions are not made.</p>
<h3>13. General rights of data subjects</h3>
<p>A data subject has the right to</p>
<ul>
<li>check what personal data is stored in the data file for him or her</li>
<li>request that incomplete personal data be completed and that Wapice rectify any inaccurate personal data without undue delay </li>
<li>have their personal data erased without undue delay, provided that
<ul>
<li>the personal data are no longer needed to the purposes for which they were collected or otherwise processed</li>
<li>the data subject withdraws his or her consent on which the processing is based, and there is no other legal ground for the processing</li>
<li>the personal data has been unlawfully processed</li>
<li>the personal data has to be erased for compliance with a legal obligation in the European Union or Member State law.</li>
</ul></li>
<li>restrict and object to the processing, if
<ul>
<li>the data subject contests the accuracy of his or her personal data</li>
<li>the processing is unlawful or the data subject opposes the erasure of his or her personal data</li>
<li>the controller no longer needs the personal data for the purposes of the processing, but the data subject needs them to prepare, present or defend a legal claim</li>
<li>the data subject has objected to the processing of personal data pursuant to Article 21(1) pending the verification whether the legitimate grounds of the controller override those of the data subject.</li>
</ul></li>
<li>withdraw his or her consent</li>
<li>withdraw his or her consent at any time, without affecting the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal, if the processing of personal data is based on the consent of the data subject.</li>
</ul>
<p>The data subject also has the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority.
A data subject may have the right to data portability if the data is such that this right is applicable.</p>
<p>The data protection officer will provide advice and guidance in matters related to the rights of data subjects.</p>
<h3>14. Other relevant information</h3>
<p>The use of the service generates log entries used to manage the security of the service and to develop the technology behind the service, as well as to detect, prevent and resolve faults (Information Society Code (917/2014), sections 138, 141, 144 and 272). The logs are retained for these purposes for as long as necessary and will not be used for any other purpose. </p>
<h3>15. Changes to the privacy notice</h3>
<p>Wapice continuously develops its operations and reserves the right to update this privacy notice. Changes may also be based on changes in legislation.
We recommend that you review this notice from time to time.</p>
<p>In the case of major operating changes resulting in material changes in the privacy notice, we may also use other means to announce the matter before its entry into force.</p>
