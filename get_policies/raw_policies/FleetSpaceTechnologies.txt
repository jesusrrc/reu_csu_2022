personal information and to whom your personal information is disclosed.</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;"><strong>What is personal information?</strong></p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">In this policy “personal information” has the meaning set out in the Privacy Act.  Essentially, personal information is information or an opinion about an individual who is reasonably identifiable.</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;"><strong>What types of personal information do we collect?</strong></p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">The types of personal information Fleet collects about you will depend on the purpose for which the information is collected.  The types of personal information that Fleet may collect generally comprises names and contact details. </p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">Fleet may also collect additional personal information such as employment history, date of birth and government related identifiers such as tax file numbers<strong>, </strong>with respect to contractors and prospective employees.  <strong> </strong></p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">Fleet does not collect sensitive information. </p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">Fleet also collects general analytics and other technical information (such as your IP address, device type, unique device identification numbers, browser type, version and language, operating system, geolocation data, browsing preferences, pages viewed while browsing the websites, page access times and referring website address and usage of Fleet's websites <a href="https://www.fleet.space/">https://www.fleet.space/</a> and https://nebula.fleet.space (<strong>Websites</strong>) or products) arising from your use of the Websites. </p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">This information is used solely internally for the purpose of gauging visitor traffic, trends and delivering personalised content to you while you are on our Websites.</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">In addition to the types of personal information identified above, Fleet may collect personal information as otherwise permitted or required by law.</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;"><strong>How do we collect personal information?</strong></p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">Fleet will usually collect your personal information directly from you, including when you:</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">·                use or subscribe to the Websites or use Fleet's products or services;</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">·                apply to work with Fleet or are engaged by Fleet as a contractor;</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">·                make inquiries about us or our services or otherwise communicate with Fleet by email, by telephone, in person, via a website or otherwise.</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">In some cases Fleet may also collect personal information from third parties such as:</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">·                publicly available sources;</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">·                entities with which Fleet conducts business; and</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">·                other third parties (for example, from referees if you apply for a position as an employee or contractor with us).</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">You may deal with us anonymously or using a pseudonym when you access many aspects of the Websites or making inquiries through the Websites or by telephone, if you do not require a response from us. However, to access Fleet's customer support features or for us to respond to inquiries, we require certain contact details from you. We generally need your personal information in order to transact with you. </p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;"><strong>Cookies</strong></p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">We use "cookies" and similar tracking technology, such as web beacons and authentication tokens on our Websites. A "cookie" is a small amount of information which is transferred to the hard drive of your computer and which can identify your web browser, but not you. </p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">Cookies can be "persistent" (cookies that stay on your browser until you delete them or else your browser deletes them based on the duration period set in the cookie) or "session" (cookies which are erased when you close your browser). Fleet uses session cookies and persistent cookies on the Websites. </p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">By using the Websites, you are consenting to the use of cookies and other tracking technologies in accordance with this Privacy Policy. </p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">If you want, you can disable your web browser from accepting cookies. If you do so, you can still access our Websites, but it may impact your user experience of the Websites. </p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">Such information does not refer to individuals by name or their contact details. We use this data in aggregate to improve our Websites and our goods and services. We may provide such aggregated data to third parties, but in so doing, we do not provide personal information without the individual's consent. </p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">Fleet uses third party providers to provide us with web analytics services, such as Google, Squarespace and Autopilot. These providers collect information on how individuals use our Websites. These providers may use cookies and other technology such as clear gifs or web beacons to obtain such information. This allows us to improve our Websites and our services. Please refer to the privacy policies of these entities on their websites, for more information on their privacy processes. </p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;"><strong>Why do we collect, use and disclose personal information?</strong></p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">In general Fleet collects, uses and discloses your personal information for purposes connected with its business operations.</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">Fleet will only collect, use, and disclose personal information about you if it has lawful basis to do so, for example: </p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">·                where you have given Fleet your consent; </p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">·                where processing of your personal information is necessary for the performance of a contract with you;</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">·                to meet Fleet's legal obligations; and </p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">·                to pursue Fleet's legitimate business interests.</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">Fleet may use or disclose your personal information:</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">·                for the purposes for which Fleet collected it (and related purposes which would be reasonably expected by you);</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">·                for other purposes to which you have consented; and</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">·                as otherwise authorised or required by law.</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">Some of the specific purposes for which Fleet collects, uses and discloses personal information are as follows:</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">·                to provide its products and services to its customers or to receive goods or services from third parties;</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">·                to enable the proper operation and functionality of its products and services;</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">·                to improve its products and services;</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">·                to verify your identity (for example, if you request access to the personal information Fleet holds about you);</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">·                to consider you for a job at Fleet (whether as an employee or contractor) or other relationships with Fleet;</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">·                to address any issues or complaints that Fleet has or you have regarding our relationship;</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">·                to comply with its legal obligations;</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">·                to develop and improve the quality of its Websites and products and customise its Websites and products according to your preferences, tailor search results, and show relevant advertising;</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">·                for direct marketing purposes (see the "Direct marketing" section below); and</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">·                to contact you regarding the above, including via electronic messaging such as SMS and email, by mail, by phone or in any other lawful manner.</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">If Fleet is unable to collect your personal information then Fleet may not be able to provide you with the products, services and opportunities that depend on the collection of that information.</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;"><strong>To whom do we disclose personal information?</strong></p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">Fleet may disclose your personal information to third parties in connection with the purposes described above.</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">This may include disclosing your personal information to the following types of third parties:</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">·                its related companies;<strong> </strong></p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">·                any potential third party acquirer of Fleet or its business or assets, and advisors to that third party;</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">·                its professional advisers (such as lawyers, accountants or auditors) and insurers;</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">·                its employees, contractors and third party service providers who assist Fleet in performing its functions and activities e.g. payment systems operators and financial institutions, cloud service providers, data storage providers, shipping companies, telecommunications providers and IT support services providers;</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">·                organisations authorised by Fleet to conduct promotional, research or marketing activities;</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">·                third parties to whom you have authorised Fleet to disclose your information (e.g. referees); and</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">·                any other person as required or permitted by law.</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">If Fleet discloses your personal information to third parties it will use reasonable commercial efforts to ensure that such third parties only use your personal information as reasonably required for the purpose of disclosure and in a manner consistent with applicable laws, for example (where commercially practical) by including suitable privacy and confidentiality clauses in Fleet's agreement with a third party service provider to which Fleet discloses your personal information.</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;"><strong>Does personal information leave Australia?</strong></p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">Fleet stores personal information in servers located in Australia and in the Netherlands as well as through third party service providers located overseas, such as in the United States of America. Fleet may disclose your personal information to overseas recipients, such as to its subsidiaries, subscribers, resellers and service providers located overseas, in order to provide its products and services and to obtain services.  Such overseas recipients may be located in different countries which are likely to include the Netherlands, the United States.   </p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">Except where an exception applies under the Privacy Act or other relevant legislation, Fleet will take reasonable steps to ensure that overseas recipients to whom Fleet discloses personal information do not breach the Australian Privacy Principles stated in the Privacy Act, and any other relevant legislation relating to privacy and data security, in relation to such information.</p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;"><strong>How do we protect your personal information?</strong></p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">Fleet engages service providers to store your personal information, including Amazon Web Services (<strong>AWS</strong>), Recurly, Autopilot and Google. Fleet recommends that you review these companies' privacy policies to understand how they use and store your personal information. </p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">Fleet will take reasonable steps to keep any personal information it holds about you secure and to protect it from unauthorised access, modification or disclosure and from misuse, interference and loss. Among other techniques, Fleet usually: </p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">·                stores information behind its "firewall" in a secure location; and </p><p class="" the security of personal information transmitted over the internet cannot be guaranteed.  Fleet cannot guarantee that any personal information you disclose to Fleet will not become publicly available.  Except to the extent liability cannot be excluded due to the operation of statute, Fleet excludes all liability (including in negligence) for the consequences of any unauthorised access section below) or by clicking on the relevant link at the bottom of any direct marketing emails. <strong> </strong></p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;"><strong>How long do we keep your Fleet not to send you direct marketing or to process your information so that Fleet can respect your request in future. </p><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">Fleet may retain your personal
