These data files may be Cookies, Flash Cookies, or other local storage provided by your browser or associated applications (collectively, "Cookies"). </p><p>Cookies are small files that store information on computers, TVs, mobile phones, or other devices. An entity that places Cookies on your device can use Cookies to identify you in different websites, services, devices, and / or browsing sessions. There are a lot of things that Cookies can do. For example: Cookies can remember your login credentials, so you don’t have to enter them every time you log in to the Service. Cookies give us and third parties an idea of which parts of our services are most popular, because they help us see which pages and functions visitors visit and how long they spend on those pages. With this information, we can improve our services more effectively and provide you with a better experience. Cookies tell us and third parties which advertisements you have watched, so that you will not receive the same advertisements every time you visit the Service. By collecting information about your use of our services as well as other websites and applications, Cookies help us and third parties provide you with relevant contents and advertisements. If you use a web browser to access services, you may configure your browser to accept all Cookies, reject all Cookies, or notify you when Cookies are sent. Each browser is different, so please check your browser's "Help" menu to learn how to change your Cookie preferences. The operating system of your device may contain other Cookie controls. </p><p>Please understand that some of our services can only be realized by using "Cookies". If allowed by your browser or add-on services thereof, you may modify the acceptance level of Cookies or reject Haier's Cookies according to your preferences. You can remove all Cookies saved on your computer. Most web browsers have the function of blocking Cookies. Please refer to the “Help” of your browser for how to modify or remove Cookies. However, this may affect your safe access to Haier-related websites and use of Haier services in certain circumstances. </p><p>Web beacons and pixel tags</p><p>Besides Cookies, we will also use other similar technologies such as web beacons and pixel tags on websites. We will collect information about your browsing activities through web beacons, such as the address of pages you visit, the address of cited pages you visited previously, how long you stayed on the pages, your browsing environment and display settings. As another example, the emails we send you may contain a clickable URL that links to the content of our websites. If you click on the link, we will track this click to help us understand your product and service preferences and improve our customer service. A web beacon is usually a transparent image embedded in a website or email. With the help of pixel tags in an email, we can find whether the email is opened or not. If you do not want your activities to be tracked in this way, you can unsubscribe from our mailing list at any time. </p><p>Mobile analysis software</p><p>In some APPs, we use mobile analysis software, such as U-App Data Statistics SDK and Haier Behavior Statistics SDK, to better understand the functioning of our APPs on your mobile phone. This software may record the following information, such as how often you use the application, events that occurred within the application, cumulative usage, performance data, and where an application crash occurred. We will not link the information stored in the analysis software to any personal information you submit in the APP. </p><p> </p><p>III. After completing these processes, you can control the device remotely or in a LAN through the APP. Some Haier devices need to be activated before they can be used. The activation process requires the use of relevant device information, including: MAC address, machine code, TYPEID, Wi-Fi password, etc. If activation fails, we may read relevant device information and log information to evaluate the cause of the activation failure. At the same time, you may need to use information such as user name / password to control your connected devices. </p><p>To enable you to find your devices. Haier may provide the "Find Device" function in some services, which can help you find and protect your device after it is lost or stolen.
