<h2><span style="color: #000000;">What are your rights in relation to your personal data?</span></h2>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">The Data Privacy Act of 2012, or DPA for short, gives you rights in relation to your personal data. It essentially gives you control on how your personal data is collected and used by companies.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Below is a list of your rights. We want to make sure that you understand what these are, so we are describing each of these rights in a simple and transparent manner:</span></p>
<ol class="normal-list style-alpha-small" id="liList7">
<li>
<p><span style="color: #000000;"><strong>The right to be informed.</strong> When we ask you to share your personal data with us, we give you details of what data we will be using, why we will be using it, and how long we will be keeping it, among other things.</span></p>
</li>
<li>
<p><span style="color: #000000;"><strong>The right to object.</strong> This is your right to tell us to stop using your personal data. Please note, however, the DPA still allows us to use your personal data despite the exercise of this right under certain conditions. For example, we will still process your personal data despite your objection if we are legally required to do so or if it is necessary to fulfill our legal obligations to you.</span></p>
</li>
<li>
<p><span style="color: #000000;"><strong>The right to access.</strong> This right allows you to ask whether we have personal data on you and, if we do, ask for a copy of that personal data.</span></p>
</li>
<li>
<p><span style="color: #000000;"><strong>The right to rectification.</strong> This gives you the right to correct anything that you think is wrong with the personal data we have on file on you.</span></p>
</li>
<li>
<p><span style="color: #000000;"><strong>The right to erasure or blocking.</strong> This gives you the right to ask us to delete your personal data. However, there are only certain instances where you can exercise this, such as in a case where you think we are processing your personal data unlawfully.</span></p>
</li>
