Please see the list of the subsidiaries by clicking <a href="https://ii-vi.com/list-of-the-subsidiaries/">https://ii-vi.com/list-of-the-subsidiaries/</a>.</p>
<p>In this Notice, we outline and describe the following with respect to the Personal Data that we collect:</p>
<ul>
<li>What information we collect and its sources</li>
<li>How we use your information</li>
<li>To whom we disclose your information</li>

<p>If you are a California resident, please see that “Additional information for California residents” section below for more information pursuant to the California Consumer Privacy Act (“CCPA”).  Additional notices, disclosures or information about Processing of Personal Data related to specific websites, products, services, or jurisdictions may be provided to you. Where applicable, these supplement and/or clarify our privacy practices in specific circumstances and provide you with additional information and choices as to how we process your Personal Data.</p>
<p><strong><u>I. What information we collect and its sources</u></strong></p>
<p>The actual Personal Data we collect and how we use it, varies depending upon the nature of our relationship and interactions with you.  Also, in some cases (such as where required by law), we ask for your consent or give you certain choices prior to collecting or using certain Personal Data.</p>
<p><strong><em>A. Information you provide directly</em></strong>:</p>
<p>We collect information (including where necessary and subject to applicable law and where required with your explicit consent, special categories of Personal Data (as defined under applicable privacy laws)) that you provide to us, our Affiliates or share on the websites, including:</p>
<p><u>Account registration and other data submitted to us online</u>: If you create a login account on any of our websites that offers this option, we ask you to provide Personal Data including your name, physical business address (including zip code), email address and phone number.  In order to use certain features of our websites, you may be prompted to provide certain Personal Data to us, such as when you fill out forms (for example, a “Contact Us” form) on our websites, downloading documentation and information from our websites, subscribe to newsletters or other communications from us, correspond with us by phone, e-mail or otherwise using our contact details.  Typically, the Personal Data includes name, business affiliation, business address, telephone number, and email address, and any other personal details provided or required to resolve inquiries or complaints.</p>
<p><u>Communications</u>: If you contact us by email, mail, phone, or otherwise regarding our products or services, we collect and maintain a record of name and contact information, contact details, your communications and our responses. If you call us, we may also record calls and maintain logs and records of those calls.</p>
<p><u>Inquiries and  requests</u>: We also collect other information about you when you choose to submit or request information from us, including through the web-form on our website or when you subscribe to our newsletter or other marketing communications from us, namely your contact information, including name, email address and phone number.</p>
<p><u>Transactions and billing information</u>: We also collect information about you in connection with a purchase you make of products or services from us. This includes your name, address, transaction data, including your transactions/purchases history, your credit card  and payment information, when you purchase a product or service from us; and your feedback regarding your purchase and the products or services that you purchased from us, if any is provided.</p>
<p><u>Customer service and support</u>: When you contact us for support or other customer service requests, we maintain support tickets and other records related to the requests.</p>
<p><u>Other information</u>: where you provide us otherwise with your Personal Data on or offline, for example at in-person events or by filling out one of our surveys.</p>
<p><strong><em>B. Information collected automatically</em></strong>:</p>
<p>We may collect Personal Data about how you use our websites and related services (e.g. by using cookies and pixel tags), such as your Internet Protocol address, location information, browser type, operating system, advertising ID, device ID, and information regarding what website pages you accessed and when, date and time of your visit to our website, and the information for which you searched and which you viewed.</p>
<p>We also use cookies and other tracking technologies to make our web services more useful to you and to tailor the experience with us to meet your special interests and needs.</p>
<p>We may also use pixels in HTML emails to understand if individuals read the emails, we send to them (“<strong>usage data</strong>”). We may combine this information with other information that we have collected about you, including, where applicable, your name and other Personal Data.</p>
<p>For information about cookies and other tracking technologies used on our website, please see our separate Cookie Notice at <a href="http://www.ii-vi.com/cookies/">http://www.ii-vi.com/cookies/.</a></p>
<p><strong><em>C. Information collected from third parties</em></strong>:</p>
<p>We may also collect information about you from third parties, such as social networks or other platforms (such as from Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn or other third party social network sites) that you may connect with or share information about us through, or when you login to our services using your login information and your user name, personal profiles, and the actual social media networks you used.</p>

<p><strong><em>Cookies</em></strong>. Cookies are alphanumeric identifiers that we transfer to your computer’s hard drive through your web browser for record-keeping purposes. Some of the Services may not work properly if you disable cookies.</p>
<p><strong><em>Clear GIFs, pixel tags and other technologies</em></strong>. Clear GIFs are tiny graphics with a unique identifier, similar in function to cookies.  In contrast to cookies, which are stored on your computer’s hard drive, clear GIFs are embedded invisibly on web pages. We may use clear GIFs (also referred to as web beacons, web bugs or pixel tags), in connection with our Services to, among other things, track the activities users of our Services, help us manage content, and compile statistics about usage of our Services. We and our service providers also use clear GIFs in HTML emails to our customers, to help us track email response rates, identify when our emails are viewed, and track whether our emails are forwarded.</p>
<p><strong><em>Third-Party analytics</em></strong>. We may use third-party analytics companies to evaluate use of our Services. <p><strong><em>Do not track (DNT)</em></strong>. Our Sites currently do not respond to “do not track” signals. You may, however, disable certain tracking mechanisms in the cookie preference settings of your web browser and opt out of certain third-party ad cookies on our websites.</p>
