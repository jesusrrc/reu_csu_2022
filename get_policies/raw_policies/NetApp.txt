<h2>Personal information in context</h2>
<n-xpm-richtext>
<p>NetApp collects personal information in a variety of contexts, each of which is defined below. The categories of information, types of information, sources of information and our purposes for collecting, processing, using, and sharing such information is determined based on this context.</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Websites</strong> NetApp collects Personal Information through our websites, including, but not limited to, the website or subdomains of <a href="https://www.netapp.com/us/index.aspx">www.netapp.com</a>, (e.g., our Communities site, Training site, and Support site) or any website that posts this policy (collectively, the “Sites”). Personal Information collected through a website, but in a different context defined in this Policy, such as the Interactions with Job Applicants Context or Products and Services context, is treated in compliance with the stated context and not the Websites Context.</li>
<li><strong>Products and Services</strong> NetApp collects Personal Information through your use of products and services made available to you by us. This includes hardware, software (including software for mobile devices or “apps”), cloud data services, professional services, and support services.</li>
<li><strong>Social Media</strong> NetApp collects Personal Information through your use of our social media pages and apps, through HTML-formatted email messages that link to this Privacy Policy, through your interactions with third party social media (such as LinkedIn, Facebook, or Twitter), and when you publicly post using NetApp-related hashtags.</li>
<li><strong>Interactions with Job Applicants</strong> NetApp collects Personal Information when you apply for a job at <a href="https://netapp.eightfold.ai/careers" target="_blank">jobs.netapp.com</a>, interact with our third-party partners’ (such as LinkedIn) postings of job openings at NetApp, or provide information to our internal or external recruiters via email or web forms.</li>
<li><strong>Market Research</strong> NetApp collects Personal Information from third party service providers for the purpose of market research, product research, targeted advertising, and business development.</li>
<li><strong>Offline</strong> When you interact in person with representatives from NetApp, such as at an event we host or participate in or when you place an order over the phone or contact customer service by phone, you may disclose or we may observe or infer Personal Information about you that you did not directly state.</li>
</ul>
</n-xpm-richtext>

<h2>Categories of types of personal information and types of information defined</h2>
<n-xpm-richtext>
<p>“Personal Information” is information relating to, directly or indirectly, an identified or identifiable natural person or household. This includes things like your name, postal address, telephone number, and email address, as well as less obvious information, which are included in the following defined categories. We plan to collect, and have collected within the preceding 12 months, the following categories of Personal Information:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Individual Identifiers</strong> This includes information like your name, email address, physical address, telephone number, login alias, IP address or Other Information that identifies you regardless of your relationship to NetApp.</li>
<li><strong>Inferences</strong> This includes customer profiles, usage preferences, or other information about an individual’s anticipated or assumed actions or behaviors.</li>
<li><strong>Customer Records</strong> This includes information like your company name, job title, account number, customer identifier, or Other Information that relates to you as a customer of NetApp.</li>
<li><strong>Education information</strong> This includes information about your educational history, such as the schools you have attended, the courses of study you followed, the status of any required continuing education for professional licensing, or Other Information relating to your education.</li>
<li><strong>Commercial Information</strong> This includes payment card data, financial account information, account information, records of products or services purchased, obtained or considered, or other purchasing or consuming histories or tendencies of the customer, and Other Information necessary to collect and process payment for products and services ordered by the customer.</li>
<li><strong>Electronic Network Information</strong> This includes information like browser and device data, and internet or network activity information, such as activity on our Websites and Services, data collected through cookies, pixel tags and other technologies, and Other Information that is generated through your use of the internet to access our websites.</li>
<li><strong>Geolocation Data</strong> This includes information such as your precise location or city, state, county, or country, if you have such geolocation data enabled when using the applicable social media application. For example, if you use Facebook to “Check In” at a NetApp-run event, we can collect that information.</li>
<li><strong>Audio, electronic, visual, or similar information</strong> This includes information that is captured on audio or video recording, such as if a telephone or video call is recorded, or simply observable in such interaction or in an Offline Context.</li>
<li><strong>Professional and Employment Related Information</strong> This includes your contact details, user name and password to be able to access the Careers Site where you can apply for jobs, and information about your current or past employers, memberships in professional associations or trade unions, and professional licensing status if you make such information publicly available in the context from which the Personal Information is collected, such as in your publicly available social media accounts, through a resume submitted to us, or by attending a professional event associated with an affinity group. This may also include your work authorization status, skills, information relating to references and any other information you elect to provide to us (e.g., employment preferences, willingness to relocate, current salary, desired salary, awards or professional memberships).</li>
<li><strong>Sensitive Information Protected under Law</strong> This includes characteristics of protected classifications under law, such as information about your age, race, religion, gender, gender identity, sexual preference, disability, political preference, trade union membership, philosophical views, marital status, medical or health information, genetic or biometric information, biometric templates, veteran status, background check information, judicial data or information on other judicial or administrative proceedings, or other pieces of information of particularly sensitive nature, if you make such information publicly available in the context from which the Personal Information is collected, such as in your publicly available social media accounts, through a resume submitted to us, or by attending a professional event associated with an affinity group</li>
</ul>
<p>“Other Information” means any information that does not reveal your specific identity or does not directly relate to an identifiable individual, such as: browser and device data, App usage data, data collected through cookies, pixel tags and other technologies, demographic information and other data provided by you that does not reveal your specific identity, or other types of information that has been aggregated in a manner such that it no longer reveals your specific identity.</p>
</n-xpm-richtext>

<h2>“Cookies” - information stored automatically on your computer</h2>
<n-xpm-richtext>

When you revisit <a href="https://www.netapp.com/us/index.aspx">www.netapp.com</a>, our server recognizes the cookie, giving us information about your prior visits. Most browsers accept cookies automatically, but you can alter the settings of your browser to prevent automatic acceptance. If you choose not to receive cookies, you can still use most of the features of the NetApp Platform, including ordering items. We do not currently respond to browser do-not-track signals.</p><p>We work with companies, such as Adobe, that use third-party cookies and Web beacons to collect anonymous information that helps us measure and improve our content and advertising effectiveness. This service may also collect information regarding the use of other websites, apps and online resources. You can learn about Google’s practices by viewing their <a href="https://policies.google.com/privacy/partners" target="_blank">Privacy &amp; Terms</a>, and exercise the opt out offered by <a href="/company/legal/cookie-policy" title="SEO cookie policy">NetApp Cookie Policy</a>.</p>

<n-prose-segment data-ntap-analytics-heading="Privacy Policy" id="GifsBeaconsBugs">
<h2>Clear gifs (web beacons, web bugs)</h2>
<n-xpm-richtext>
<p>We employ a software technology called clear gifs (also known as Web beacons or Web bugs) along with other technologies such as e-tags, and JavaScript that help us better manage content on our site by informing us what content is effective. Clear gifs are tiny graphics with a unique identifier, similar in function to cookies that are used to track the online movements of Web users. In contrast to cookies, which are stored on a user’s computer hard drive, clear gifs, which are about the size of a period, are embedded invisibly on Web pages. For detailed information regarding NetApp’s use of Clear Gifs and other technologies, please refer to the <a href="/company/legal/cookie-policy" title="SEO cookie policy">NetApp Cookie Policy</a>.</p>
</n-xpm-richtext>
