<p>This Privacy Notice describes how Software AG and its affiliates (hereinafter “Software AG,” “we,” or “us”) process personal data. It applies if you:</p>

<li>participate in Software AG’s forums, portals and other events or</li>
<li>are another data subject from whom Software AG receives personal data.</li>
</ul>
<p>This Privacy Notice only applies when Software AG processes personal data in its role as a data controller. It does not apply if Software AG processes personal data as a processor.</p>
<p>Software AG may change this Privacy Notice at any time. Software AG provides access to archived prior versions via the link above in this Privacy Notice.</p>

<p><b>1. Who is responsible for your data and how to contact Software AG’s data protection officer?</b><br/>
<b>Software AG, Uhlandstraße 12, D-64297 Darmstadt, Germany</b> is the data controller in accordance with Article 4 of the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (“GDPR”).</p>
<p>Software AG’s Data Protection Officer can be reached by email at dataprotection@softwareag.com or by post at the following address:</p>
<p><b>Data Protection Officer <br/>
 Software AG <br/>
 Uhlandstraße 12<br/>
 D-64297 Darmstadt<br/>
 Germany</b></p>
<p><b>2. Which kinds of personal data does Software AG process?<br/>
</b>Software AG collects personal data directly from you, your contacts and public sources.</p>
<p>Customers and suppliers may provide personal data to Software AG. In this case, the customer or supplier ensures that the personal data is transferred to Software in accordance with applicable data protection law.</p>
<p>Below you will find a list of the categories of personal data which Software AG processes</p>
<ul>
<li>Computer Internet Protocol (IP) address, date and time of use, operating system and information on the Internet browser used including installed add-ons, internet address of the website from which the online offer was accessed (so-called origin or referrer URL) ("device data");</li>
<li>Name, e-mail, employer, business address, profession, gender, contact details ("business data");</li>
<li>Name, username, passwords ("account login data");</li>
<li>Personal data of employees and other contacts of customers, billing details ("customer data");</li>
<li>Event registrations and participation, user behavior, service requests, downloads ("marketing data");</li>
<li>Communication data, documents and materials exchanged in the course of conducting customer business ("conduct data");</li>
<li>CVs, cover letters and other data exchanged in connection with a job application to Software AG ("applicant data");</li>
<li>Passports or other identification documents, date of birth ("verification data").</li>
</ul>
<p>Special categories of personal data, such as health data, trade union membership, religious affiliation, will only be processed if required by law or if you have given your consent. </p>
<p><b>3. For which purposes do we process data and on what legal basis?<br/>
</b>Software AG processes personal data for the following purposes and on the following legal bases:</p>
<p>In order to provide services, equipment, business, account login, customer data are necessary to fulfil the contracts concluded with the customers. The legal basis in this case are the legitimate interests of Software AG.</p>
<p>In order to perform internal business processes as well as customer and supplier management, business, account login and customer data are required in order to fulfil the contracts concluded with the customers/suppliers. The legal basis in this case are the legitimate interests of Software AG.</p>
<p>Marketing, business, account login and device data are processed to provide information about interesting events, products and services, technical and commercial news, customer conferences, forums, portals, other networking opportunities and to be able to answer your requests to us. The legal basis in this case are the legitimate interests of Software AG. In some countries, your prior consent to marketing activities is required and will be requested in the appropriate way.</p>
<p>Making the Software AG's websites as user-friendly as possible in order to offer and provide products and services that meet your needs requires device data. The legal basis in this case are the legitimate interests of Software AG or your consent.</p>

<p>To comply with legal requirements, business, verification, account login, execution and device data are needed to comply with Software AG’s legal obligations.</p>
<p>For applicant management, applicant and verification data is required in order to fill vacancies at Software AG. On the one hand, this is necessary to establish an employment relationship and is also within in the legitimate interests of Software AG. On the other hand, we process this data to comply with legal obligations of Software AG.</p>
<p>In order to enable you to use our portals and forums, account login data is required to perform the services of the portals and forums. The legal basis in this case are the legitimate interests of Software AG.</p>
<p>In order to be able to publish your contributions in the forums of Software AG, account login data is required, which is within the legitimate interests of Software AG or based on your consent. <p><b>4. How does Software AG process your personal data by using cookies?<br/>
</b>Through the use of cookies and related technologies ("cookies"), we process data, which may include personal data, for advertising and analysis purposes, to tailor our website to your interests, to display or send you information about us where necessary with your consent, and to keep you informed about our products and services that may be of interest to you. We also use cookies to understand how our services are used so that we can improve them. For example, we use cookies for analytics and diagnostic purposes to improve our products and services, and to measure and analyze the use and performance of our services. Based on this technology, we may receive evaluations from our service providers, which may include personal data - if you have given your consent – or aggregated data. Aggregated data cannot be used to draw any conclusions about your person.</p>
<p>Below you will find general descriptions of the types of cookies used on our websites, including the purposes and legal basis.</p>
<p><b>Required cookies <br/>
</b>Necessary cookies enable navigation and basic functions of the websites, e.g. access to protected areas of the websites. The legal basis for processing information using necessary cookies is our legitimate interest.</p>
<p><b>Functional cookies <br/>
</b>Functional cookies allow us to analyze your use of the website and your defaults on the website (for example, your login name, language or region) so that we can remember these preferences and provide you with a more personalized experience on our websites. These cookies may also be used to remember settings such as font size, font type and other configurations you have made on the websites. The legal basis for processing information through functional cookies is your consent.</p>
<p><b>Advertising cookies<br/>

This may result in cookies being set by the service providers to track the performance of our advertising. In addition, we have agreements with advertising networks. This may result in data about you being processed across multiple browsing devices, such as your PC or laptop, smartphone or tablet. Our third-party service providers use this information to serve advertisements for products and services based on your personal interests. This may result in advertisements being displayed on each of your browsing devices. The advertising cookies and related services help us analyze and monitor the effectiveness of our marketing activities. The legal basis for the processing of information through advertising cookies is your consent.</p>
<p><b>Cookie settings<br/>
